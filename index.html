My Process

I believe that good design lies at the intersection of human desirability, market viability and technical feasibility. When I approach any new project, my design process consists of 3 main steps.

Ideate

  • Empathize
  • User interviews
  • Field obervations
  • Problem definition

Create

  • Competitive Analysis
  • Management plan formation
  • Prototyping
  • Usability Testing

Iterate

  • Gather feedback
  • More prototyping!
  • Hope that the idea works
  • Launch product

LIFE SKILLS MINIGAME

This Unity based game aims at teaching Financial Management to underprivileged children. It features a coin-collecting game, where a customizable avatar runs and jumps to collect coins while avoiding obstacles. The coins collected can be managed by an online banking interface that allows players to pay bills and manage funds.

VSHARE

VShare is an app for the apple watch and allows the user to exchange business cards, contact information, etc with other apple watch users just through a handshake! The app eliminates the common job seeker's effort of carrying multiple resumes and at the same time, allows recruiters a platform for storing resumes.

PROXIMITY

Getting bored at a DMV or waiting for your Starbucks coffee? Proximity is here for you! Proximity is a Facebook Messenger feature designed to facilitate interest-based conversations between and among people within a radius of 1,000 ft. This is done by entering into group chats, which consist of only people around you, if the topic of that group is interesting to you.

Data Analysis: Obesity in the United States

Programmed using iPython

Sugared beverages, french fries and hamburgers: beloved staples of Western diets yet insidious threats to long-term health. Obesity in the United States is an epidemic, yet many dismiss it as a problem caused by unfavorable genetics or lack of willpower.

In particular, the idea has gained traction that lack of desire to exercise is the root cause behind obesity, culminating in political programs encouraging exercise as a remedy, such as Michelle Obama’s “Just Move”. A lot of research exists exploring the link between lifestyle and obesity, though in many epidemiological studies, proxy features such as "caloric intake in kcal" are used.

Yet such calorie and exercise-focused models may not adequately consider how food environments shape behavior as root causes. To what extent do socio-economic context and food behaviors (e.g. income level and frequency of consuming sugared soda) predict obesity? Investigating this question may reveal that food environments and dieting behaviors serve as better predictors of obesity than exercise-related features.
